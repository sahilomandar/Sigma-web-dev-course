The earliest noted adventures of humans with coding take us back to 60 A.D. Heron of Alexander was a Greek mathematician and engineer. He invented the machine that was used to control puppets with the help of strings. The machine could be reprogrammed by rearranging the strings to make the puppets do different things.

The first computer programming language was created in 1883, when a woman named Ada Lovelace worked with Charles Babbage on his very early mechanical computer, the Analytical Engine.

While the names of the coding paradigms sometimes vary, most experts agree on four primary types of code: imperative, functional, logical, and object-oriented. Alternative names and other primary types may include procedural, scripting, and database programming.

FORTRAN (FORmula TRANslation) is the oldest, most universal language for programming computer systems. It was created in 1957 and is still used in many scientific and numerical applications.
